# Stillwater Area Public Schools (ISD 834) — News Coverage, 2015–2026

*A compiled record of public news coverage of Independent School District No. 834
(Stillwater Area Public Schools, Washington County, Minnesota) over roughly the
last decade. Prepared July 2026 by the Stillwater Schools Finance Project from
public web sources.*

> **Scope & method.** Items were gathered via public web search in July 2026 and
> organized by theme, then chronologically. Each entry links to a primary
> article. Dates are taken from the articles themselves; where a date is
> approximate it is marked "~". Some local-news links (Stillwater Gazette /
> hometownsource, Star Tribune, Patch) may be paywalled or may move over time.
>
> **Disambiguation.** There is also a *Stillwater Public Schools* in **Oklahoma**.
> Sources covering the Oklahoma district (e.g., `stwnewspress.com`, `ocolly.com`)
> are **excluded** here; everything below concerns the **Minnesota** district
> (ISD 834). This is a coverage index, not an endorsement of any outlet.

## 1. The "BOLD" plan and the elementary school closures (2015–2017)

The defining episode of the decade. In December 2015 the district unveiled
**"Building Opportunities to Learn and Discover" (BOLD)**, a strategic plan that
proposed closing three elementary schools — **Marine, Withrow, and Oak Park** —
and consolidating students into the remaining schools, citing declining
enrollment, under-capacity buildings, and budget pressure. After a lengthy public
hearing, the board voted **5–2 on March 3, 2016** to close the three schools. The
decision drew intense community opposition, organized petitions, and a legal
challenge by the nonprofit **834 VOICE**, which was ultimately unsuccessful.

## 2. Leadership and governance

Superintendent **Denise Pontrelli** was hired in 2015 (and led the district
through the BOLD closures); she departed in mid-2020 under a separation
agreement, with the board citing a breakdown in trust and communication.
**Malinda Lansfeldt** served as interim superintendent for two years. In April
2022 the board hired **Michael (Mike) Funk**, then superintendent of Albert Lea
Area Schools and the 2022 Minnesota Superintendent of the Year; he began July 1,
2022.

## 3. COVID-19: closures and learning models (2020–2021)

Like all Minnesota districts, ISD 834 closed under Governor Walz's March 2020
order and moved to distance learning. It opened 2020–21 in a hybrid model and
shifted secondary students fully online in November 2020 as Washington County
case rates rose.

Voters approved a replacement operating levy (~**56.4%** yes; ~$14.7M/year for 10

### 2023 building bond — $175 million (Nov 7, 2023)

Voters approved a $175M bond (**~56.7%** yes) to replace Lake Elmo and Andersen
elementary schools, expand Oak-Land Middle School, and fund district-wide
safety/security. The measure carried a ~20-year payoff (~$16/month on a
$500,000-value home). Board member Lauer was elected the same night.

## 5. Facilities and enrollment growth (2023–2026)

With the 2023 bond approved, the district selected sites for replacement
elementary schools — a new Lake Elmo Elementary (Lake Elmo Avenue & 10th Street
North) and a relocated Andersen Elementary near Barker's Alps Park in Bayport —
and began reviewing attendance boundaries. Lake Elmo is among Minnesota's
fastest-growing cities, and the district projects enrollment growth over the next
decade.

## 6. Budget pressures and Priority-Based Budgeting (2024–2026)

After years of thin operating reserves, the district turned to a **Priority-Based
Budgeting (PBB)** process to close recurring gaps — about **$5M (~3% of the
operating budget) for 2025–26** and a projected **~$5.5M for 2026–27** — citing
inflation, the expiration of federal pandemic grants, uncertain state funding, and
unfunded mandates such as the READ Act literacy requirement. Reported cuts fell
heaviest on special-education and instructional-support positions.

- [District To Close $5 Million Budget Gap for 2025-26](https://www.stillwaterschools.org/our-district/news/district-news-detail/~board/district-news/post/district-to-close-5-million-budget-gap-for-2025-26) — ISD 834 (district)
