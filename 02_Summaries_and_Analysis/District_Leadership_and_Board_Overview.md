# Stillwater Area Public Schools (ISD 834) — Leadership & Board Overview

*Prepared July 2026 by the Stillwater Schools Finance Project from public sources.*

This overview summarizes the district's **senior leadership** and **Board of
Education**, then applies a transparent **assessment rubric** — a checklist of
attributes you would generally want to see in strong district leadership and
governance — and indicates, for each person or body, which criteria are
**publicly evidenced**.

> **How to read this — important.** The rubric below is a *civic-governance
> checklist*, not a performance review or a judgment of anyone's competence or
> character. A mark reflects only what is **documented in public sources**.
> Many board members have limited public professional biographies; where an
> attribute is simply not on the public record it is marked `n.d.`
> ("not documented"), which is **not** a negative finding. Officers and
> administrators are public officials; the descriptions here are factual and
> sourced. **Rosters, officer titles, and terms change** — verify the current
> board against the district's official page before relying on it.
>
> **Legend:** `✓` publicly evidenced · `◐` partial / indirect evidence ·
> `n.d.` not publicly documented.

## Part 3 — The rubric

Two rubrics: one for **executive leadership** (superintendent + cabinet) and one
for the **board** (both as individual directors and as a governing body). The
criteria are drawn from widely used governance guidance (e.g., Minnesota School
Boards Association effective-governance principles and general nonprofit/public
board best practice).

### A. Executive-leadership criteria

| # | Criterion | What "meets" looks like |
|---|-----------|-------------------------|
| L1 | **Credentials** | Advanced, role-relevant credential (Ed.D./Ph.D., MBA, CPA, licensure). |
| L2 | **Senior experience** | Prior senior leadership in a comparable role/organization. |
| L3 | **Track record / recognition** | Documented outcomes or external recognition. |
| L4 | **Functional fit** | Expertise matches the specific portfolio (e.g., CFO ← accounting/finance). |
| L5 | **Stability** | Permanent (not interim); reasonable expected tenure. |
| L6 | **Cabinet completeness** | Full functional coverage across the leadership team. |

### B. Board criteria

**Individual director criteria:**

| # | Criterion | What "meets" looks like |
|---|-----------|-------------------------|
| D1 | **Officer role** | Holds a defined office (chair/vice/clerk/treasurer). |
| D2 | **Governance/civic experience** | Prior board, commission, or public-service experience. |
| D3 | **Relevant expertise** | Professional expertise useful to oversight (finance, education, law, ops, health). |
| D4 | **Community ties** | Documented local/community engagement. |
| D5 | **Tenure / continuity** | More than one term or clear institutional knowledge. |
| D6 | **Public record / transparency** | Candidate statements or public record readily available. |

**Whole-board (governance body) criteria:**

| # | Criterion | What "meets" looks like |
|---|-----------|-------------------------|
| G1 | **Complete officer structure** | Chair, vice chair, clerk, treasurer all filled. |
| G2 | **Staggered terms / continuity** | Terms staggered so the board never turns over at once. |
| G3 | **Fiscal-oversight framework** | Fund-balance policy, annual audit review, budget process. |
| G4 | **Strategic plan & superintendent evaluation** | Adopted goals; regular superintendent evaluation. |
| G5 | **Transparency** | Published agendas/packets/minutes; open meetings. |
| G6 | **Diversity of expertise** | Range of professional/background perspectives. |

## Part 5 — Summary observations

- **Leadership is stable and credentialed again.** After a two-year interim
  period (2020–2022), both the superintendent and CFO seats are held by
  experienced, permanent appointees — directly relevant given the district's thin
  operating reserves and multi-year budget gaps.
- **The finance function is well-matched to the challenge.** A long-tenured
  public-school CFO paired with the Priority-Based Budgeting process is the right
  structural response to the reserve/deficit pressures documented elsewhere in
  this repository.
- **Board structure is sound; individual transparency is thin.** The board meets
  the structural governance criteria (officers, staggered terms, fiscal framework,
  published materials), but public professional biographies for most directors are
  limited — the clearest area where additional public information (candidate
  statements, board member bios) would allow a fuller assessment.
- **Biggest open question:** a publicly visible, regular **superintendent
  evaluation** and clearly published **strategic-plan progress reporting** would
  strengthen the governance picture.
