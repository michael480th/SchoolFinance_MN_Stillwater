# Minnesota's K-12 Education Subtraction and Credit

## K-12 Education Subtraction

Minnesota allows a state income tax subtraction (deduction) for K-12 education-related expenses.
- Up to **$2,500** for each dependent in grades 7-12
- Up to **$1,625** for each dependent in grades K-6

**Tax year 2023 usage:**
- 112,500 returns claimed the subtraction
- Total statewide tax reduction: $13.07 million
- Average tax reduction: $107

---

## K-12 Education Credit

Minnesota allows income taxpayers to claim a **refundable tax credit** equal to 75% of certain K-12 education-related expenses.
- Capped at **$1,500** for each child in grades K-12
- For taxpayers with two or more qualifying children, the $1,500/child cap is a **family limit**

**Tax year 2023 usage:**
- 38,000 returns claimed the credit
- Total credits claimed: $17.21 million
- Average credit: $453

**Phase-out (beginning tax year 2026):**
- Begins phasing out when income exceeds **$77,550**
- Reduced by 25% or 50% of AGI above that amount (depends on number of children)
- Fully phased out at $83,550 (one or two children)
- Extends additional $3,000 of income for each additional child

---

## Eligible Expenses

Nonpublic school tuition is an eligible expense for the **subtraction only** (not the credit).

Both subtraction and credit allow:
- After-school enrichment, academic summer camps, music lessons, tutoring
- Textbooks and instructional materials
- Transportation costs (paid to others for transporting children to school)
- School supplies, musical instrument rental/purchase, up to $200 of computer hardware and educational software
