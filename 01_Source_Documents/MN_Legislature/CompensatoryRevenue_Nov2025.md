## Overview

Compensatory revenue is the state's primary formula designed to provide additional funding to school districts serving students from lower-income families. Minnesota's compensatory formula is based on the previous year's count of free and reduced-price meal-eligible students, which serves as a proxy for the number of students in need of additional educational assistance.

---

## Compensatory Revenue Formula History

- Since 1969 MN has had a compensatory revenue component in school finance formulas
- Original: $30/pupil in districts where >20% students from AFDC families
- "Minnesota Miracle" (1971): AFDC revenue scaled up as part of Foundation Revenue Program — added 0.5 pupils to overall pupil count ("total pupil units")
- 1987 open enrollment + 1991 charter schools increased student movement; better proxy measures investigated
- Mid-1990s: federal AFDC replaced by TANF — state changed from districtwide AFDC count to **site-based count of students eligible for free or reduced-price meals**
- FRL count + concentration formula unchanged for ~30 years since

---

## Key Formula Changes (Recent)

- **Fall 2022**: Direct certification expanded to include Medical Assistance eligibility → substantial increase in eligible students
- **Fall 2024**: For FY2026+, count based **only on direct certification** (not individual applications for educational benefits)

---

## Student Count Details

**Counting Compensatory Pupils:**
- Pupil counted as compensatory if eligible for free or reduced-price meals under federal school lunch program
- Free meals: ≤130% of federal poverty guidelines
- Reduced-price meals: 130–185% of federal poverty guidelines
- For 2024-25: free meal income limit = $40,560/year (family of 4); reduced = $57,720/year
- Count conducted in fall of previous year at each school site
- October 1 enrollment date; schools have until December 15 to determine eligibility
- Direct certification: SNAP, Medical Assistance (added fall 2022), other public assistance programs

---

## Revenue Formula

**Site-Based Revenue:**
- Calculated per school site; revenue designated to that site
- Board may adopt resolution to reallocate up to 20% of site revenue across district

**Concentration Formula:**
- As concentration of FRL-eligible students increases → compensatory revenue per compensatory pupil also increases

**Eligible Uses:**
- Must be used to meet educational needs of pupils whose progress toward state/local standards is below age-appropriate levels
- Specific eligible uses listed in Minnesota Statutes

---

## Trends Over Time

- Eligible students increased from 2000s through ~2018
- Dropped significantly during COVID (2020, 2021) — federal free meals for all disrupted tracking
- Increased dramatically FY2024 — Medical Assistance added to direct certification (fall 2022)
- Compensatory revenue FY2003–FY2025 actual; FY2026–2029 from February 2025 Forecast
