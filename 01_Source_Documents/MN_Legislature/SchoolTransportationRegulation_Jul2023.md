## Overview

This brief outlines Minnesota Statutes governing school-related transportation. Key regulatory areas:
- Driver qualifications (licensing, background checks, drug testing)
- Driver and pupil training
- Vehicle registration, taxation, and tax exemptions
- Vehicle and equipment standards
- Passenger restraints
- Traffic regulations specific to pupil transportation
- Loss of driving privileges

Transportation regulation varies depending on: (1) vehicle type, (2) whether transport is to/from school or for activities, and (3) whether driver is employed specifically to transport students.

**Jurisdiction:** State of Minnesota has primary jurisdiction over school bus equipment, safety, operations, inspections, district auditing, and driver licensing. Multiple agencies involved:
- **Department of Public Safety** (State Patrol): primary responsibility over transportation safety (Office of Pupil Transportation Safety); Minn. Stat. § 169.435
- **Department of Education (MDE)**: administers transportation-related state funding; involved in transportation operations and policy
- **Driver and Vehicle Services (DPS)**: driver licensing, physical exams, background checks

## School District Powers and Duties

### Mandatory Transportation (Minn. Stat. § 123B.88, subd. 1)
- School districts must transport any student to and from school if the student lives **more than two miles from school**
- Districts must provide **equal transportation for nonpublic school children** residing within district boundaries
- Additional required transportation:
  - Students with disabilities when included in their IEP
  - Nonresident pupils attending through open enrollment (from serving district's border to the school)
  - Resident pupils attending a charter school within the district if the charter school declined to provide transportation

### Charter School Transportation (Minn. Stat. § 124E.15)
- Charter schools are subject to the same two-mile mandate as school districts for students living within the borders of the district where the charter school is located
- A charter school may: (1) arrange transportation itself, OR (2) require the district to provide the service and forgo related transportation funding

### District Discretion
- Districts may provide transportation beyond minimums (within two-mile radius, extracurricular activities)
- Districts may operate own fleet, contract with private vendor, or use combination

## Key Finance-Relevant Notes

1. **Transportation funding**: MDE administers state transportation aid to school districts. See MDE Data Center TOPICID=47 for transportation revenue/expenditure data.
2. **Tax exemptions**: Traditional school buses receive certain vehicle registration and tax exemptions not applicable to general vehicles
3. **Two-mile mandate**: Creates mandatory expenditure obligation for ISD 834 — any student living >2 miles from school must be transported at district expense
4. **Nonpublic school transportation**: District must provide equal transportation for nonpublic school students within district boundaries — additional cost obligation for ISD 834
5. **Charter school bill-back**: If a charter school within ISD 834 declines to arrange its own transportation, ISD 834 must provide it and the charter forfeits related transportation funding
6. **Special education transportation**: Required when specified in IEP — funded separately through special education revenue, not general transportation aid
