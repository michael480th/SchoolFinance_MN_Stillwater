## Minnesota Comprehensive Assessments (MCAs)

Students in Minnesota public schools (including charter schools) take standardized state assessments known as MCAs, aligned to state academic standards.

### MCA Schedule by Grade and Subject

| Subject | Grades Tested |
|---------|--------------|
| Reading | Grades 3–8 + once in high school |
| Math | Grades 3–8 + once in high school |
| Science | Grades 5, 8 + once in high school |

- MCAs fulfill federal accountability requirements under **ESSA** (Every Student Succeeds Act)
- Computer-based online assessments; grades 3–8 are **computer-adaptive** (difficulty adjusts based on responses)
- Minnesota's ESSA plan: https://education.mn.gov/MDE/dse/ESSA/mnstp/

### Alternate Assessment — MTAS
- **Minnesota Test of Academic Skills (MTAS)**: for students with the most significant cognitive disabilities
- ESSA caps MTAS participation at **1% of all students tested** in any subject

## English Learner Assessment Requirements

- English learners take MCAs starting in first year of enrollment
  - Year 1: take MCAs but results **not included** in accountability calculations
  - Year 2: scores used to calculate academic **progress**
  - Year 3: scores used to calculate both **progress and achievement**
- **ACCESS**: assessment measuring progress toward English language proficiency
- **Alternate ACCESS**: for English learners receiving special education services

### Translated Assessment Materials Available
- Pop-up translations and word lists: Spanish, Somali, Hmong (three most widely spoken non-English languages)
- Word lists also available in: Amharic, Arabic, Chinese, Karen, Khmer, Laotian, Oromo, Russian, Vietnamese

## Reading/Literacy Screening

- Schools must administer MDE-approved literacy screener to students in **kindergarten through grade 3**, three times each school year
- Screeners assess mastery of foundational reading skills and identify students demonstrating characteristics of dyslexia
- Schools must report results to the commissioner annually

---

## Opting Out of Assessments

- Students may opt out of state standardized assessments
- MDE must create and publish an opt-out form (parent-initiated)
- ESSA requires states to annually measure achievement of **at least 95% of all students** and 95% of each subgroup
- Minnesota's **"North Star" accountability system**: a student who opts out is counted the same as a student who is "not proficient"
- The percentage "not proficient" is one of several factors used to identify schools for support

---

## Local Assessments

Districts and charter schools may require locally adopted assessments. Annual time limits:
- Grades 1–6: **10 hours maximum**
- Grades 7–12: **11 hours maximum**

## ISD 834 Assessment Context

- MCA results for ISD 834 available on MN Report Card: https://rc.education.mn.gov/ (district: 27-834-01)
- Proficiency rates affect school accountability status and federal Title I allocations
- ACT/SAT fee reimbursements: state appropriates funds annually to reimburse districts for FRL-eligible students
- Literacy screener results must be reported to MDE — potential UFARS coding for related expenditures
