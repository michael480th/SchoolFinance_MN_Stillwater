## Executive Summary

State law requires teachers in public schools to hold a license aligned to their field and grade level. The traditional pathway involves a bachelor's or master's degree in education plus licensure exams, but alternative pathways (portfolio, apprenticeship, Grow Your Own) have developed over time. The state does not set teacher salaries — these are determined locally through collective bargaining or school board action, funded through general education revenue. This publication summarizes licensure requirements and state programs to recruit teachers.

## Teacher Licensing System

### Tiered Licensure (Established 2017)
PELSB (Professional Educator Licensing Standards and Board) issues licenses under four tiers per Minn. Stat. §§ 122A.181–122A.184:

| Tier | Duration | Renewal | Portability |
|------|----------|---------|-------------|
| Tier 1 | 1 year | Limited renewals | Only at district/school that applied |
| Tier 2 | 2 years | Limited renewals | Only at district/school that applied |
| Tier 3 | 3 years | Indefinitely renewable | Any school district or charter school |
| Tier 4 | 4 years | Indefinitely renewable | Any school district or charter school |

- Tier 3 and Tier 4 are "professional licenses"
- Out-of-field permission: allows teaching outside licensure area for one academic year
- PELSB rules for field licenses: Minnesota Rules, part 8710.3000–8710.5850

## Programs to Expand the Pool of Teacher Candidates

### Grow Your Own (GYO) Grants (Minn. Stat. § 122A.73)

**Type 1 — Adult Pathway to Teaching License:**
- Grant to district, charter school, Tribal contract school, or cooperative unit
- At least 80% of funds must provide tuition scholarships/stipends for paraprofessionals or community members of color or American Indian to complete teacher prep programs
- Funds may also cover licensure exams and fees
- Can be structured to allow candidates to continue working while completing coursework
- District may require up to 5 years of teaching in exchange

**Type 2 — Secondary School Students:**
- Supports future teacher clubs, service-learning, dual enrollment "Introduction to Teaching" courses
- Provides scholarships to graduating HS students of color or American Indian for teacher prep programs
- Eligibility: district must ensure % of students of color/AI in program ≥ overall % in district

### Collaborative Urban and Greater Minnesota Educators of Color (CUGMEC) Program (Minn. Stat. § 122A.635)
- Competitive grants to IHEs for teacher prep programs targeting candidates of color or American Indian seeking Tier 3 license
- Administered by PELSB; criteria include graduation and licensure recommendation rates
- Originally "Collaborative Urban Educator (CUE)" grant, created 1997; renamed 2017; codified 2019

### Expanded Concurrent Enrollment Grants (Minn. Stat. § 124D.09, subd. 10, para. (b))
- Schools/districts partnering with a PSI to offer "Introduction to Teaching" or "Introduction to Education" concurrent enrollment courses
- Students earn both HS and college credit
- Goal: encourage students (especially American Indian and students of color) to consider teaching

### Grants to Prepare American Indian Teachers (Minn. Stat. § 122A.63)
- Awards to partnerships of school districts and PSIs to assist American Indian people to become teachers
- Named partnerships (with additional funding if available):
  - UMN Duluth + ISD 709 Duluth
  - Bemidji State University + ISD 38 Red Lake
  - MN State University Moorhead + district in White Earth Reservation
  - Augsburg College + ISD 625 St. Paul + Special School District No. 1 Minneapolis
- At least 80% must fund student scholarships; priority to enrolled Tribal members, then first/second-generation descendants

### Pathway Preparation Grants (Laws 2023, ch. 55, art. 5, § 65, subd. 7)
- Grants to support Tier 2 teachers seeking Tier 3 license through teacher prep programs or portfolio process
- Eligible: school districts, charter schools, service cooperatives, and partnerships
- Recipients must provide professional development, mentorship, and appropriate coursework

### Black Men Teach Twin Cities (Laws 2021 and 2023)
- Grant to Black Men Teach Twin Cities organization
- Goal: increase Black male teachers to 20% of teachers at each partner school site
- Funds: scholarships for aspiring teachers, student teacher stipends, mentoring, professional development, housing stipends

### Alternative Teacher Preparation Grant Program (Minn. Stat. § 136A.1276)
- Grants to districts, charter schools, or qualifying nonprofits for alternative/experimental teacher prep programs
- Must certify working to fill MN teacher shortage areas
- Uses: PELSB approval, program expansion, recruiting diverse candidates, professional development
- Last appropriated 2017

### Coalition to Increase Teachers of Color and American Indian Teachers (Laws 2023)
- Grant to Coalition for nonlobbying activities supporting recruitment and retention of racially/ethnically diverse teachers

### Teacher Residency Program (Laws 2023)
- Year-long clinical experience integrating learning opportunities and student teaching
- Cohort-based model; residents paired with cooperating teachers
- Each candidate placed for full academic year; at least 80% of contracted school week co-teaching
- Cooperating teacher is teacher of record; receives ongoing professional development

## Support for Student Teachers

- Tier 3/4 candidates must complete 12 weeks of full-time student teaching (generally unpaid)

### Underrepresented Student Teacher Grant (Minn. Stat. § 136A.1274)
- Stipend up to $7,500; administered by Office of Higher Education
- Requirements: enrolled in PELSB-approved program (12+ weeks student teaching required), demonstrate financial need, meet satisfactory academic progress, belong to racial/ethnic group underrepresented in MN teacher workforce

### Student Teachers Grants in Shortage Areas (Minn. Stat. § 136A.1275)
- Stipend up to $7,500; same requirements except:
- Must intend to teach in rural school district OR in a license shortage area (ESL, early childhood, special ed, CTE, STEAM, world languages)
- "Rural school district" = fewer than 30 resident pupils per square mile

### Pilot Program (Laws 2024, ch. 115, art. 5, § 20)
- Effective 2024-2025 school year only
- Eight specified providers: St. Cloud State, Bemidji State, MN State Mankato, Winona State, Fond du Lac Tribal, UMN Duluth, UMN Crookston, Augsburg
- Each provider received specified funding; must award equal stipend to each student teacher regardless of financial need or licensure area
- Only for student teachers placed in MN school district or charter school
- Stackable with other grant programs

## Programs to Support Teachers in Specific Fields

### Special Education
- Consistently highest proportion of Tier 1/Tier 2 teachers and out-of-field permissions
- 2024 legislative changes: limit Tier 1 special ed teachers to no more than 3 years; must receive professional development and intensive supervision; applicant must demonstrate satisfactory progress toward professional licensure

### Special Education Teacher Pipeline Program (Minn. Stat. § 122A.77)
- Grants to districts, charter schools, Tribal contract schools, cooperative units
- Support persons already employed who wish to become special ed teachers
- Partners with IHEs or programs with transfer pathways
- Priority: training Tier 1/Tier 2 to achieve Tier 3 special ed license
- Equal grants to greater MN and metro applicants (where possible)

### Special Education Apprenticeship Programs (Laws 2024)
- Grants to four intermediate school districts for special ed registered apprenticeship programs
- Funds: program oversight, stipends, tuition, fees, mentor stipends, substitute teacher costs

### Heritage Language and Culture Teachers (Minn. Stat. § 122A.631)
- Yearlong mentorship program for heritage language/culture teachers
- Portfolio creation, monthly meetings, stipend for substitute teacher coverage
- Funding includes portfolio liaison, substitute teacher fees, and licensure fees

### Agricultural Educator Grants
- Grants to districts to pay agricultural education teacher salary/benefits for summer extended programs with HS students
- Grantee must provide 1:1 dollar match
- Last appropriated: Laws 2017, 1st spec. sess. ch. 5, art. 2, § 51

## Direct Financial Support for Teachers and Candidates

### Tax Credits
- Nonrefundable tax credit up to $2,500 for a teacher who completes a master's degree in a core content area (reading, ELA, math, science, foreign languages, civics, economics, arts, history, geography) (Minn. Stat. § 290.0686)
- Pedagogy-only master's does not qualify

### Teacher Shortage Loan Repayment (Minn. Stat. § 136A.1791)
- Up to $1,000/year for up to 5 years for qualified educational loans
- Disbursed directly to participants by Commissioner of Higher Education
- Eligibility: belong to racial/ethnic group underrepresented in MN teacher workforce
- Also eligible if in rural district or license shortage area (if funds available)
- "Underrepresented" = group where % of MN teachers < % of K-12 students of that group

### Aspiring Teachers of Color Scholarship Pilot Program (Laws 2021)
- Up to $10,000/year, max $25,000 total
- For undergrad/grad students in PELSB-approved teacher prep programs seeking first license, or 2-year early childhood programs
- Must self-identify as person of color or American Indian, demonstrate financial need, meet satisfactory academic progress

### Reimbursements for Teacher Licensing and Exam Fees (Laws 2023)
- Covers teacher licensing exam costs and first professional teacher license fees for newly graduated teachers

### Hiring Bonuses (Laws 2021 and 2023) (Minn. Stat. § 122A.59)
- Reimbursement to districts/schools for bonuses given to teachers from another state or country
- Requirements: immediately qualify for Tier 2+; moved to MN; belong to underrepresented racial/ethnic group
- Amount: $4,000–$10,000 (higher for shortage areas); second half requires Tier 3/4 license

### Teacher Recruitment Marketing Campaign (Laws 2021 and 2023)
- Funds to PELSB for outreach/marketing campaigns via competitive RFP process
- Target audiences: HS/college students of color or AI who haven't chosen a career; adults of color or AI seeking career change
- Goal: elevate teaching profession and recruit diverse teachers

## Teacher Workforce Data Sources

- **PELSB STAR System** (Staff Automated Reporting): licensure and employment data from districts and charter schools; includes licensed and nonlicensed staff and assignments
- **PELSB Supply & Demand Report**: published biennially; retirement patterns, licensure, diversity, teacher shortage areas, substitute shortages
- **PELSB Diversity Report**: summary of state programs to increase racial/ethnic diversity of teacher workforce; due November 3, 2025, then every even-numbered year
- **U.S. Department of Education Title II Report**: enrollment in teacher prep programs by gender and race/ethnicity, admission/completion requirements, pass rates on credentialing assessments — https://title2.ed.gov/Public/Home.aspx
