# Dual Enrollment Programs for High School Students

## Executive Summary

Minnesota law authorizes two types of dual enrollment programs: Postsecondary Enrollment Options (PSEO) and concurrent enrollment (also called dual credit or concurrent credit). Both programs allow secondary school students to earn postsecondary credit while still in high school. PSEO provides postsecondary credits only, while concurrent enrollment provides both secondary and postsecondary credits simultaneously.

---

## Postsecondary Enrollment Options (PSEO)

### Overview
- Authorized under Minnesota Statutes, section 124D.09
- Allows eligible secondary students to enroll in nonsectarian postsecondary courses at eligible postsecondary institutions (PSIs)
- Students earn postsecondary credit only (not high school credit) for PSEO coursework
- Full-time PSEO: student takes all courses at PSI, not at district
- Part-time PSEO: student takes some courses at PSI, some at district

### Eligible Students
- Grades 10, 11, and 12 for most courses
- Grades 9 and 10 for courses in career and technical education (CTE)
- Pupil must be enrolled in a public school district or charter school, or be a nonpublic school student or homeschool student
- PSI determines academic eligibility

### Eligible Institutions
- Public postsecondary institutions (U of M, MnSCU)
- Private nonsectarian colleges and universities
- Tribal colleges

### PSEO Funding Formula

**Full-time PSEO students (enrolled entirely at PSI):**
- MDE pays PSI 88% of the basic general education revenue the district would have received for that student
- District retains 12% of basic general education revenue
- "Other types of revenue, including compensatory revenue, English learner revenue, and special education revenue, are 'saved' by the state" — these are not paid to the PSI and not retained by the district

**Part-time PSEO students:**
- PSI receives a proportionate share of general education revenue based on the proportion of time the student spends at the PSI
- District receives the remaining proportionate share

**Nonpublic/homeschool students in PSEO:**
- MDE pays PSI 88% of basic general education revenue
- Nothing is paid to a school district (nonpublic students don't generate revenue for a district in the first place)

### Key Policy Points
- Districts must allow PSEO enrollment; they cannot prohibit eligible students from participating
- PSI provides textbooks and materials at no cost to the student
- Transportation to the PSI is the student's and family's responsibility (not district-funded)
- Districts may not assess a student's performance in a PSEO course

### FY2022 PSEO Data (Statewide)
- 11,189 students participated in PSEO
- 172,605 credits earned through PSEO

## Concurrent Enrollment

### Overview
- Students enroll in college-level courses taught at the secondary school by high school teachers
- Students earn both secondary (high school) and postsecondary credit simultaneously
- Authorized under Minnesota Statutes, section 124D.091
- Also called "concurrent credit" or "dual credit"

### Key Features
- Courses taught by high school teachers (who must meet PSI faculty qualifications)
- Offered at the high school during the regular school day
- High school teachers must be approved by the PSI as adjunct faculty
- PSI determines course standards, content, and rigor

### Eligible Students
- Any secondary student (generally grades 9-12)
- PSI may set additional eligibility requirements

### Concurrent Enrollment Funding
- Concurrent enrollment courses are funded through the district's general education revenue
- No separate state aid formula specifically for concurrent enrollment costs
- Districts pay fees to the PSI for the partnership; typically $50-$150 per credit per student
- State provides "Expanded Concurrent Enrollment Grants" to offset some costs (competitive grants)

### FY2022 Concurrent Enrollment Data (Statewide)
- 32,505 students participated in concurrent enrollment

---

## Comparison: PSEO vs. Concurrent Enrollment

| Feature | PSEO | Concurrent Enrollment |
|---------|------|-----------------------|
| Where taught | PSI campus | High school |
| Instructor | PSI faculty | HS teacher (PSI-approved adjunct) |
| Credit earned | Postsecondary only | Both secondary AND postsecondary |
| Transportation | Student's responsibility | N/A (at HS) |
| Books/materials | PSI provides free | Varies |
| Funding impact on district | Reduces district gen ed revenue (88% to PSI) | No direct revenue reduction; district pays PSI fee |

## Related Programs

### College in the Schools (CIS)
- Established program where University of Minnesota offers courses through high schools
- High school teachers teach U of M approved courses

### Expanded Concurrent Enrollment Grants
- Competitive grants to encourage more schools, especially those serving American Indian students and students of color, to participate in concurrent enrollment
- Focus on "Introduction to Teaching" or "Introduction to Education" courses to build teacher pipeline

---

## Implications for ISD 834 (Stillwater)

- PSEO enrollment reduces district general education revenue: for each full-time PSEO student, the district receives only 12% of basic general education revenue (versus 100% if the student remained at the district school)
- Revenue for PSEO students that is "saved by the state" includes compensatory revenue, EL revenue, and special education revenue — these do not flow to either the district or the PSI
- District retains categorical revenues tied to PSEO students (e.g., if a PSEO student is also special ed, the district may still receive some special education revenue for IEP services)
- Growing PSEO participation statewide creates revenue pressure on districts with high PSEO rates
